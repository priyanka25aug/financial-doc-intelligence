RISK FACTORS

The following risk factors may materially adversely affect our business, financial condition, and results of operations. Investors should carefully consider these risk factors.

1. MARKET RISK
We are exposed to significant market risk arising from our trading activities. Elevated VIX readings and market volatility have increased mark-to-market losses on certain derivatives positions. Our Value at Risk (VaR) model may not capture all market risk under extreme conditions.

2. CREDIT RISK
Counterparty credit risk represents a material adverse factor. Default by a significant counterparty could result in substantial losses. We hold collateral agreements with major counterparties, but collateral may prove insufficient under stress scenarios.

3. REGULATORY RISK
We are subject to extensive regulation by the SEC, FINRA, and international regulatory bodies. Non-compliance with capital adequacy requirements could result in enforcement actions, fines, or restrictions on business activities. Forward-looking regulatory changes represent an uncertainty that could affect our operating model.

4. LITIGATION RISK
We are party to several pending legal proceedings. Adverse outcomes could result in material financial liability. See Note 14 to the financial statements for details on litigation matters.

These forward-looking statements involve risks and uncertainties that could cause actual results to differ materially from those anticipated.
